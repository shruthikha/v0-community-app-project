# Nido — Decision Principles

> **How to fill this out:** Decision principles are the default tiebreakers when product decisions are ambiguous. The product agent uses these to make calls without you in the loop.
>
> **Process:**
> 1. Reflect on the last 5 hard product decisions you made — what principle did you actually use? Sometimes principles are already in your head; you've just never written them down.
> 2. Reflect on decisions you keep deferring because they're hard — what's making them hard? Often the difficulty is because no principle exists yet. Writing the principle clears the path.
> 3. Identify what you would ALWAYS choose, even if it costs you. ("Always choose privacy over convenience even when users ask for the convenient thing.")
> 4. Identify what you would NEVER do, even if it would grow the product faster. ("Never show ads.")
> 5. Aim for 5-10 principles. Each should be a clear default ("prefer X over Y"), falsifiable (you can imagine evidence that would change it), and specific to Nido.

---

## How to use this document

When facing a product decision and the right answer isn't obvious, check these principles in order. The first one that applies is the tiebreaker. If none apply, escalate to the user (MJ).

The product agent and orchestrator both reference this document during `/spec`, `/plan`, and design discussions.
